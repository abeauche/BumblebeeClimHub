#### BEE NEST ANALYSIS ####
BEEBOX has the most reliable detections of nest departures/returns with minimal noise.
- High quality audio recordings, minimal environmental noise due to close proximity to nest entrance and shelter from wind.
- High confidence in behaviour detected --> nest departure/return vs foraging or other behaviours.
- Good TOMST coverage (TOMST logger close by in exact same aspect and env. conditions).
- Proximity to other climate dataloggers.

#### Data Compilation & Processing ----
Data sources: 
- 2024: ARUQ recordings, TOMST data, weather station data (air rh, atm pressure), ECCC weather station (wind speed, wind dir), photoperiod.
- 2025: ARUQ recordings, TOMST data, weather station data (air rh, atm pressure, precipitation, solar radiation, wind dir, wind speed, gust speed), photoperiod.

Notes: precipitation data unreliable, consider y/n, radiation only available for 2025

Compile 2025 dataset:
- Summarise weather station data to the hourly resolution, taking mean of values recorded on shorter intervals.
- Extrapolate buzz detections to the hourly resolution (sum two 10-minute detection estimates, multiply by 3 per hour).

#### QUESTION 2: What is the effective temperature threshold for bumblebee activity at the nest?
Using 2024 + 2025 data?

Modelling a temperature-performance curve (TPC) --> https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-024-53046-2 
- Importance of comparing the performance of multiple alternative models across a given dataset, rather than selecting a single model in an arbitrary manner. (Kontopoulos et al. (2024))
- Phenomenological models may be better suited for quantifying parameters related to the shape of the TPC, such as critical thermal limits (Tmin and Tmax)
- Definitely a phenomenological modelling approach for this study -> unable to directly link temperature to enzyme action.
- Because of high-resolution dataset, consider parameter-rich model.
- If the goal is to estimate the thermal optimum, there should be numerous data points before and after the peak of the TPC. If this is not the case, the parameter estimates may be imprecise and biased to various degrees
- Only consider positive values for response (ie activity)
- Interested in Tmin, Tpk, and Tmax
- Package nls.multistart fits model from different starting values
  - Possibly: Specify starting values by performing linear regression on a subset of the dataset (e.g., to estimate the slope of the rising part of the TPC) or by using parameter estimates from the literature. 
  - Fit each model to thermal performance dataset 1000 times. In every iteration, use a different starting value per parameter, sampling from a uniform distribution with bounds equal to 0.5 and 1.5 times initial starting value.
